The Dark Tower

2017 Directed by Nikolaj Arcel

Synopsis

There are other worlds than these

The last Gunslinger, Roland Deschain, has been locked in an eternal battle with Walter O’Dim, also known as the Man in Black, determined to prevent him from toppling the Dark Tower, which holds the universe together. With the fate of the worlds at stake, good and evil will collide in the ultimate battle as only Roland can defend the Tower from the Man in Black.

  • Review by Jared ★ 6

    Not really worth discussing in relation to it's brilliant source material, because the essence of the story is absent from this movie. The menace of the Man in Black is nonexistant, the depth of the Gunslinger was left untapped, and the spiritual myth behind the Dark Tower is not even introduced. It's a rushed bastardization of a great literary work with an overqualified, wasted cast. All of it's narrative progressions are dependent on better versions of similar motifs from other films, none of it's "sincere" parts are earned and the whole story and the characterization of King's legendary creations is awful. This is a dry, worn out husk of an adaptation and woefully uninspired sixty-six million dollar misfire.
